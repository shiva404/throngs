  BANK RECONCILIATION — Q4 2024:

  Business Operating Account (City National Bank):
  October and November reconciled clean. 134 and 97 transactions respectively.
  December: 141 transactions. I started the reconciliation and hit a $14,700 difference.

  A $14,700 discrepancy at December 31.

  This is not a rounding error. This is not a timing difference. This is wrong and I
  need to find it.

  I spent 47 minutes debugging the December reconciliation. I am going to document this
  because it was an excellent example of systematic debugging:

  Step 1: Check that the opening balance matches November's ending balance. ✓ It does.
  Step 2: Confirm all December deposits match the bank statement. Found it on pass 3:
  a $14,700 deposit on December 22 is in the bank statement but NOT in QB. The bank
  shows "WIRE CREDIT — SETTLEMENT FUNDS 12/22." Patricia received a settlement payment
  from a case and it was deposited directly to the operating account. She did not record
  it in QB. The wire hit the bank but there is no corresponding revenue entry.

  This is a significant omission. A $14,700 unreported revenue item. If this had sailed
  through to the tax return without being caught, it would have been underreported income.

  I called Patricia immediately. She confirmed: yes, that's the Hendricks v. Pacific
  settlement, her firm's contingency fee for a case that settled December 20. She had
  the settlement in her calendar but forgot to tell me or record it. She'd assumed "QB
  would pick it up from the bank feed." QB does pick up bank feed transactions — but
  she has QB set to "manual approval" mode for the operating account, meaning transactions
  don't auto-enter until approved. She saw the wire in the bank feed queue but hadn't
  approved it.

  I want to be clear: this is not QB's fault. This is a user workflow issue. Patricia
  has her bank feed set to require manual approval, which is the right setting for an
  attorney trust account (IOLTA) but less critical for the operating account. I've
  suggested she switch the operating account to auto-enter. She hasn't.

  But here's the QB element: the bank feed approval queue had 47 pending items in it
  when I opened it. The $14,700 wire was buried in the middle of the queue, listed as
  "Bank of America Wire" with no additional description. Not flagged. Not highlighted.
  QB's UI treats a $14,700 wire credit identically to a $4.99 Spotify charge in the
  pending queue — same row, same visual weight. A high-value, unusual transaction type
  (wire credit) should surface differently. This is a reasonable UX criticism.

  Approved the transaction. Categorized as Legal Fees Earned. Reconciliation cleared.
  Difference: $0.00.

  Then I ran the AR aging report. Bloom Legal has $67,400 in outstanding receivables.
