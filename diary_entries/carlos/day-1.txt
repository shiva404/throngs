  ⬛ QUICKBOOKS ONLINE SESSION 2 (CLIENT: Chen's Bakery)  [10:20 AM – 11:50 AM, 90 min]

  Linda Chen has owned the bakery for 11 years. Her books are meticulous compared to most
  of my clients. She categorizes her own transactions throughout the month and I review
  and adjust. This makes my job significantly easier.

  Reviewed her self-categorized transactions first. 102 transactions in February.
  Found 6 issues:
  1. Loan payment of $2,340 booked entirely to "Loan Payment" as an expense. This is
     the most common client error I see. Split it properly: $1,847 to principal (balance
     sheet reduction of liability), $493 to interest expense (P&L). Corrected.
  2. New commercial mixer purchased for $3,800 — she booked it as "Kitchen Supplies."
     This is a capital asset. Created a new fixed asset in QB, recorded the purchase,
     set up straight-line depreciation over 7 years ($542.86/year, $45.24/month). The
     depreciation journal entry won't show until I run it end of period.
  3. Small one: $47 from Amazon she called "Advertising." It was printer paper and ink.
     Office Supplies.
  4. Three transactions from her flour/sugar supplier (Golden State Bakery Supply)
     she categorized as Cost of Goods Sold — correct, but she used the wrong sub-account.
     She used "Food & Beverage" when I have her set up with "Raw Ingredients." Minor,
     but I keep her accounts consistent for year-end.
  5. Refund from PG&E for $214. She left it uncategorized. Credited it back to Utilities.
  6. The loan payment issue I already mentioned was the big one.

  Reconciliation: Linda's bank account — First Republic business checking. 118 items.
  Cleared clean. Difference $0.00. Her savings account also reconciled. Clean.

  Then: the QB upgrade prompt appeared while I was trying to run her Advanced Reporting
  "Profit & Loss by Product/Service" view. A banner slid across the top of the screen:
  "Unlock deeper insights with QuickBooks Online Advanced — upgrade today!"

  I am on QuickBooks Online Accountant. This is the accountant-tier product. I have

  Linda is on QuickBooks Plus. This report IS available to her. The banner was incorrect.

  I cannot stress enough: I know this software better than most of the people who built it.
