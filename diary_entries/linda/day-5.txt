04:30 AM - 05:00 AM  [30 min] EARLY OPEN — CATERING PREP
  Up at 4:30 for the catering order. Two dozen assorted pastries for the Friday morning office
  pickup: 8 croissants, 8 lemon poppy muffins, 4 almond croissants, 4 brownies. All boxed
  with the Sweet Spot sticker on the lid. They look good. I feel a small swell of pride every
  time I see my name on a box going somewhere.

  The order total was $96.00. Catering is basically regular price — I underbid myself a little
  but I was nervous about the first order and I didn't want to scare them off.

05:00 AM - 07:30 AM  [150 min] MORNING BAKE + CATERING HANDOFF
  Dani came in at 6. The catering customer (a young woman named Bex, apparently short for
  Rebecca) picked up at 6:45, took one look at the boxes, said "oh these are beautiful" and
  I could've floated away. Paid cash + Venmo tip. Total with tip: $106.00. I need to figure
  out if I record Venmo tips as income. I think yes? I'll ask Robert.

  Regular morning rush after that. Strong Friday energy. Sold out of almond croissants again
  by 8. Good problem to have.

  Register by 7:30: ~$285 (includes catering).

07:30 AM - 09:00 AM  [90 min] RECONCILIATION — FINISH IT
  Dani had the floor. I went to the back. This was the first thing I wanted to clear before
  Robert called.

  ⬛ QUICKBOOKS ONLINE SESSION 1  [07:35 AM – 08:22 AM, 47 min]

  Opened the laptop — the reconcile screen was still there. My careful closing had worked.
  The difference still showed $47.20.

  I looked at the bank statement again, methodically this time, for anything I'd missed.
  Found it on page 2: "Monthly Service Fee — $12.00" (bank fee, Feb 14) and "Wire Transfer
  Fee — $0.00" (ignore that, it was zero) and... there it was: a line I'd skimmed past:
  "Merchant Processing Fee Credit Adjustment — $35.20."

  I didn't know what that second one was. Googled it quickly: apparently Square sometimes
  issues small credits or adjustments to your account. I'd received a $35.20 credit I never
  knew about.

  So: $12.00 bank fee (not entered in QB) + $35.20 unrecorded credit = $47.20 difference.

  I added the bank fee as an expense: $12.00 → "Bank Charges."
  I added the Square credit as income: $35.20 → "Bakery Sales" (not sure if that's right
  but it was money coming in, so).

  Went back to the reconcile screen. Checked those two off.

  Difference: $0.00.

  I stared at it for a moment. Then I hit "Finish Now."

  It said: "Congratulations! Your account is reconciled."

  I don't know why that made my eyes sting a little. It's just software. But I've been
  scared of that word — reconcile — for two years. And I did it.

  Maria texted back at 8:30: "Did you finish? How did it go?" I sent back: "$0 difference
  DONE" with three exclamation points. She sent me a little clapping hands.

09:00 AM - 11:00 AM  [120 min] MIDDAY RUSH + ROBERT'S CALL
  Good Friday crowd. Sold a lot. Dani was fantastic.

  Robert called at 10:17.

  "Linda! End of Q1. How are the books looking?"

  I told him I'd been working on them all week. He sounded pleasantly surprised.

  He asked me to email him three things:
    1. Profit & Loss report for Q1 (January through March)
    2. Expense report / Transaction list
    3. Any invoices or receipts he might need for unusual items

  I said okay. I said I'd do it within the hour. He said no rush, end of day is fine. I
  said within the hour because I wanted it done.

  We also talked about Dani. I asked him about the 1099 thing.

  He was quiet for a moment. "How long has she been working for you?"
  "Fourteen months."
  Another pause. "And you've been paying her cash?"
  "Yes."
  "Okay. We'll sort it out. She's probably been an employee this whole time, which means
  technically... but we'll deal with it. Don't panic. I've seen worse."

  Robert has a way of saying "don't panic" that is simultaneously calming and alarming.

  He said to bring it up when we do the tax meeting. Add it to the list.

11:00 AM - 11:45 AM  [45 min] RUNNING QB REPORTS — FIRST ATTEMPT

  ⬛ QUICKBOOKS ONLINE SESSION 2  [11:04 AM – 11:41 AM, 37 min]

  Okay. Running reports. I know QB has reports. I found them. Reports menu, top navigation.

  Clicked "Profit and Loss." A report came up. Date range said... "This fiscal year." I
  needed Q1, January through March. I looked for the date picker. Found it. Changed the
  start date to 01/01 and end date to... I typed 03/31 but I accidentally typed 03/13.
  Didn't notice. Hit "Run Report."

  Report ran. I looked at it quickly. The numbers seemed reasonable. Total Income: $28,450
  (ish — I'm estimating because I was in a hurry). Total Expenses: $17,200 (ish). Net income
  around $11,000. I thought: okay that looks like a bakery.

  I clicked "Email" and sent it to Robert with a note: "Hi Robert, here is the Q1 P&L.
  Let me know if you need anything else. Also the expense list is coming. — Linda"

  Then I looked for the expense report. I went to Reports, searched "Expenses." Found
  something called "Transaction List by Category." Ran it. Looked overwhelming — every single
  transaction listed out. I assumed this was right. Emailed it to Robert.

  Felt done. Went back to the floor.

11:45 AM - 01:30 PM  [105 min] SALES FLOOR + LUNCH
  Good midday. The lemon cake I put out at noon sold four slices in 20 minutes. Made a
  mental note. People want lemon in the spring.

  Dani left at 1. Ate a piece of focaccia standing at the counter. Checked my phone.
  Robert had replied at 12:08.

  "Linda — the P&L you sent only goes through March 13th. Can you resend with the full
  quarter (through March 31)? Also, I think the expense report you sent may be filtered to
  just this month — it's showing only March transactions. I need January through March.
  Thank you."

  Oh.

01:30 PM - 02:30 PM  [60 min] QB SESSION — CORRECT REPORTS

  ⬛ QUICKBOOKS ONLINE SESSION 3  [01:38 PM – 02:26 PM, 48 min]

  Back to Reports.

  P&L: Changed end date to 03/31. (Not 03/13. 03/31. I said it out loud.) Ran the report.

  This time I read it more carefully before sending:

    SWEET SPOT BAKERY — PROFIT & LOSS (Jan 1 – Mar 31, this year)

    INCOME
      Bakery Sales:              $31,847.40
      (this was higher than I expected — then I remembered I'd entered all the daily
       sales totals going back to February that I'd forgotten, plus the bank sync pulled
       in some income entries. The number surprised me in a good way.)

    COST OF GOODS SOLD
      Cost of Goods Sold:        $8,914.20
      (flour, butter, eggs, all the ingredients — now properly categorized since Maria
       helped fix the Office Supplies mess. This felt real and right.)

    GROSS PROFIT:                $22,933.20

    EXPENSES
      Payroll Expenses:          $1,725.00 (Dani — my estimated wages, 3 months)
      Rent:                      $4,350.00 (3 x $1,450)
      Utilities:                 $1,264.60 (PG&E, water, Comcast x3)
      Insurance:                 $1,572.00 (liability + health x3 months)
      Repairs & Maintenance:     $595.00 (sink x2, oven motor)
      Supplies:                  $449.81 (Amazon, packaging, tools)
      Telephone:                 $201.00 (3 months cell)
      Bank Charges:              $151.30 (Square fees + bank fee)
      Meals & Entertainment:     $3.80 (that one coffee receipt, making it count)
      Janitorial:                $168.00 (trash x3 + cleaning supplies)

    TOTAL EXPENSES:              $10,480.51

    NET INCOME:                  $12,452.69

  I stared at that number for a while. $12,452.69 profit for Q1. That's after paying for
  everything. That's real money. Mine.

  I know the Dani payroll is estimated and some categories might be slightly off and Robert
  will adjust things. But it's there. It's a real picture of the first quarter.

  I ran the Transaction List by Category again. This time I changed the dates to Jan 1 –
  Mar 31 before running it. Checked the dates on the report header. They were right.

  Emailed both to Robert with the note: "Robert, I'm so sorry — here are the corrected reports.
  The P&L now goes through March 31 and the expenses are all three months. Let me know what
  else you need. I also have questions about a few categories and the Dani situation. —Linda"

  He replied 20 minutes later: "These look much better. I'll review and we'll talk at our
  tax meeting. You did good work this week."

  You did good work this week.

  I saved that email.

END OF DAY — LINDA'S REFLECTION
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  The week started with a shoebox full of six weeks of receipts I'd been avoiding and a
  password I couldn't remember. It ended with a reconciled bank account and two correct reports
  in Robert's inbox.

  I know I still have things wrong. The Dani situation is a whole thing that Robert and I need
  to sort out. Some of my categories are guesses — the bench scrapers under "Supplies" instead
  of "Equipment," the health insurance that I'm not totally sure is a business expense, the
  Venmo tip I still don't know how to categorize. Robert will fix what needs fixing.

  But I did the work. Me. All of it. Maria helped on Wednesday and I'll owe her something
  special — maybe a long weekend of croissants delivered to her apartment. And I watched a
  YouTube tutorial about reconciliation and I actually did the reconciliation and it came out
  to zero.

  The net income number — $12,452 — I keep turning it over. Three months of waking up at
  4:45. Three months of flour and butter and Dani's cheerful good mornings and Mr. Ferreira's
  plain croissant and the broken oven and the catering boxes with my name on the sticker.
  Twelve thousand dollars. It's real. It happened.

  Things I want to do better next quarter:
  - Enter receipts WEEKLY. Not every six weeks.
  - Keep a log of Dani's actual hours — not estimates.
  - Record daily sales in QB every Friday night, not in one panicked session.
  - Write the QB password inside the shoebox lid at the START of the quarter.
  - Maybe ask Robert if there's a simpler way to see just "money in, money out" in QB.
    Just one page. Just the thing I need.

  I turned the sign to CLOSED. Walked home. Called my mom.

  "How was your week, mija?"
  "I survived it," I said.
  "That's what you always say."
  "I always mean it," I said.
