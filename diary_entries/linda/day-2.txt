  SWEET SPOT BAKERY — OWNER DIARY
  Linda Torres | Tuesday, Week 1
  "Some days the bakery wins. Today the bakery destroyed me."
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

DAY SUMMARY
───────────
Total hours worked:      13h 45min
Customers served:        ~39 (lost count during the oven crisis)
QB Online sessions:      1  (22 min total — rough session)
Receipts entered today:  5 (with at least 2 mistakes I know of)
Oven emergencies:        1
Times I almost cried:    2

04:45 AM - 05:30 AM  [45 min] OPEN BAKERY / PREP
  Started my usual routine. Sourdoughs in, cases wiped, espresso machine warmed up. Noticed
  the convection oven was making that grinding noise again - worse than yesterday. I turned it
  off and back on and it seemed okay. Told myself it was fine. It was not fine.

05:30 AM - 07:15 AM  [105 min] MORNING BAKE — THEN DISASTER
  I had two sheet pans of croissants in the big convection oven at 6:20. The oven started making
  a sound like a shopping cart with a broken wheel, then - nothing. Fan stopped. Heat just gone.
  I opened the door: half-baked croissants. Pale, doughy, not even close.

  I cannot describe the feeling of watching $60-70 of product just sitting there, undone.

  Pulled them out. Tried to finish them in my smaller countertop oven - only fits one pan at a
  time and it runs hot on the right side. The first batch came out uneven. Sold what I could.
  Threw out about 14 croissants that weren't presentable. Called the repair company - they
  said "tomorrow or Thursday at the earliest." I said some words I won't write down.

  Dani doesn't work Tuesdays. So I was alone.

07:15 AM - 09:30 AM  [135 min] DAMAGE CONTROL / REDUCED OPENING
  Put a handwritten sign on the door: "Limited pastry selection today - we appreciate your
  patience!" Ran on muffins, scones, and the focaccia from yesterday. A few people were
  disappointed about the croissants. Mrs. Okafor said she'd come back Wednesday. I gave her
  a free scone for the trouble. I know I shouldn't keep giving things away but I couldn't help it.

  Register running slow - by 9:30 only about $140.

09:30 AM - 11:00 AM  [90 min] EMERGENCY BAKE ADAPTATION
  Shifted to things I can make in the smaller oven or on the stovetop. Did two batches of
  snickerdoodles, a pan of brownies, some shortbread. It's not my usual lineup but it's something.
  My feet were killing me. Forgot to eat breakfast.

11:00 AM - 01:00 PM  [120 min] SALES FLOOR + PHONE CALLS
  Called my supplier to make sure Thursday's flour delivery was still on. Confirmed. Called
  my landlord to ask (again) about the electrical situation - the oven repair guy last time
  said the voltage in the kitchen was "inconsistent." Landlord said he'd "look into it."
  That's his favorite phrase.

  A catering inquiry came in via email - someone wants two dozen assorted pastries for a
  Friday morning office thing. I said yes. Added it to my calendar. Then remembered I have no
  working convection oven. Texted the repair shop again.

  Lunch total around $310. Not terrible given the circumstances, but $80-100 off a normal Tuesday.

01:00 PM - 03:30 PM  [150 min] AFTERNOON + CLEANUP
  Things slowed down. Sold out of the brownies, which made me feel better. Made a note to put
  brownies on the regular menu. Cleaned the oven (the broken one) like that would somehow help
  it. Swept. Wiped. Counted the drawer: $387.00 for the day. I usually do closer to $475 on
  Tuesdays. That's almost $90 gone.

  I did rough math on the croissant loss: about 14 at $3.50 each = $49. Plus the time, the
  stress, the repair bill coming. I felt tired in a bone-deep way.

03:30 PM - 05:30 PM  [120 min] SUPPLY RUN + HOME
  Drove to Restaurant Depot for butter (ran lower than expected) and heavy cream. Spent $61.40.
  Got home. Sat on the couch. Told myself I'd do QB after dinner.

05:30 PM - 06:30 PM  [60 min] DINNER
  My neighbor Gloria knocked to drop off tamales (bless her). We talked for 45 minutes on the
  porch. I probably needed that more than I knew. She asked how business was going. I said "the
  oven broke." She said "that's the bakery talking." We laughed.

06:30 PM - 07:00 PM  [30 min] FAILED QB ATTEMPT
  Sat down at the table. Opened QB. The dashboard again. I noticed it said my checking account
  hadn't been updated since... February 18th? That seems like a long time. I don't know how
  that updates. Does it sync automatically? Did I set that up? I honestly don't remember.

  I got distracted trying to figure out the bank sync thing and ended up on the wrong page
  (something called "Chart of Accounts") which had all these intimidating words: General
  Ledger, Accounts Receivable, Equity, Retained Earnings. I felt like I was reading a foreign
  language. Closed the tab. Sat there for a minute. Opened it back up.

07:00 PM - 07:00 PM  → Called Maria (my daughter). She's 27, works in tech, very patient with me
  about computers. She said she'd come over Wednesday evening after work. "Mom just don't touch
  anything big until I get there." I promised.

07:00 PM - 09:00 PM  [120 min] QB — LATE NIGHT (BAD IDEA)
  Couldn't sleep anyway. Decided to just do receipts, nothing complicated.

  I'm going to let Maria look at it tomorrow.

END OF DAY — LINDA'S REFLECTION
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  What a day. The oven, the lost croissants, the reduced sales, the tamales from Gloria saving
  what was left of my evening. $387 on a day that should've been $475+. And the repair bill is
  coming. I don't even want to guess what a commercial convection oven repair costs.

  I got 5 more receipts into QB but honestly I shouldn't have done it that tired. I think I
  duplicated the Restaurant Depot entry. And I know the food supply stuff is in the wrong
  category - I've been doing it wrong for months and I just keep doing it wrong because I don't
  know how to fix it all.

  Maria coming tomorrow is the best news of the week. She's going to sigh at me a little but
  she'll help and she won't make me feel stupid about it. I love that kid.

  The catering order Friday is still on. I need the oven back. Please let the oven come back.
