06:15 PM - 08:25 PM  [130 min] QB SESSION WITH MARIA
  Maria arrived at 6:15 with a coffee for herself and a very patient expression.

  ⬛ QUICKBOOKS ONLINE SESSION 2  [06:18 PM – 08:25 PM, 127 min]

  Maria took one look at the Chart of Accounts and made a small sound. Not a bad sound. Just
  an "I understand what I'm looking at" sound.

  THE OFFICE SUPPLIES PROBLEM:
  She found 18 transactions under "Office Supplies" that should've been under "Cost of Goods
  Sold." Flour, sugar, butter from Gordon's and Sysco going back to January — and she clicked
  "Last 90 days" and found some from December too, which means I've been doing this since
  before the new year. Three months of food supplies categorized as office supplies.

  "Mom, this is actually fixable," she said. She showed me how to open each transaction and
  change the category. We did them one by one. It took 35 minutes. I felt embarrassed but she
  kept saying "this is really common" and I think she meant it.

  THE BANK SYNC:
  She found the bank connection settings. Apparently the sync had disconnected - she said it
  happens sometimes, she just needed to re-enter my bank login credentials through Intuit's
  connector. She did that. The sync ran and pulled in about 6 weeks of transactions. Suddenly
  there were a LOT of new items showing "For Review." Some of them I'd already entered manually,
  so there WERE duplicates. We spent 20 minutes going through them and excluding the ones I'd
  already entered.

  THE DANI QUESTION:
  Maria looked at how I'd entered Dani's wages. She said if Dani is a regular part-time
  employee (same days every week, long-term), I should probably have her set up as an employee
  in QB's payroll section or at minimum be issuing her a 1099 at year-end. I... didn't know
  that. I've been paying her cash for 14 months. Maria said to ask Robert about it. I wrote
  it down: ASK ROBERT ABOUT DANI.

  THE DASHBOARD CONFUSION:
  Maria explained why the profit number doesn't match the bank account. She said something
  about "cash basis" vs. something else, and also about how QB is tracking all the transactions
  including ones that haven't hit the bank yet, or... honestly she explained it clearly and I
  nodded and I think I understood for about 90 seconds and then it blurred. The short version
  she gave me: "The dashboard profit is what you've earned on paper. Your bank is what's
  actually sitting there. They're different because of timing." Okay. I can live with that.

  By 8:25 we had:
  - 18 recategorized transactions
  - Bank sync reconnected
  - Most duplicates resolved
  - 6 more receipts entered (Maria helped me do them fast)

  She said: "Mom you actually don't have that many more receipts to do. You're close."
  I nearly hugged her for that. I did hug her for that.
