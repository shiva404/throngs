12:15 PM - 12:45 PM  [30 min] CUSTOMER COMPLAINT — Damaged book
  Customer (Mr. Reeves, regular, 3rd purchase this month) bought a
  hardcover on Monday. He's opened it at home to find the spine has
  a printing defect — the pages aren't trimmed evenly, bottom 30 pages
  slightly misaligned. Publisher issue, not our handling.

  He was annoyed but polite. I:
  1. Apologised sincerely and immediately (his perception of the
     situation matters more than the technical cause)
  2. Checked our stock: one remaining copy — same batch, same defect.
     This is a publisher printing run issue.
  3. Offered: full replacement when new stock arrives (Ingram order
     due tomorrow/Monday) or full refund now.
  4. He chose refund. Processed: $38.99 refund to his card.
  5. Entered QB credit/refund transaction against original sale.
  6. Filed a returns claim with Ingram for 2 defective units
     (the returned one + the one still on shelf). Emailed photos
     of the defect to Ingram customer service.

  He left satisfied. Said he'd be back. I believe him.
  *** ACTION ITEM: Flag defective batch in QB notes. Check if
  there's a pattern — 2 defects in same batch might mean more. ***
