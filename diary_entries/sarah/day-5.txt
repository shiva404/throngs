8:52 AM - 11:15 AM  [143 min] MORNING FLOOR — 6 transactions
  Friday mornings are a specific kind of quiet. Not dead — just
  purposeful. The people who come in on Friday morning know what they
  want. They're treating themselves after the week.

  1. Regular, older man (I think his name is Gerald but I've never
     been confident enough to confirm it — he'd probably find it odd

  2. Young woman, new customer — came in specifically because she'd
     seen the window display photo on Instagram. She stood in front of
     the window for a full minute reading it from outside before coming
     in. Bought "The Kite Runner" from the display (she'd been
     meaning to read it for "basically my whole adult life") and added
     a new poetry collection from our front table on a whim. $27.98.

     look into it properly. Third time this month someone's asked.

  3. A couple, browsing together. This is one of my favourite things to
     watch — couples in bookshops either agree on everything or make
     it immediately clear they have completely different inner lives.
     These two disagreed pleasantly about everything and each bought

  4. Phone-in order: a customer (Mrs. Okonkwo, a regular) called asking
     if we had "James" by Percival Everett in paperback. We do —
     one copy. I held it under her name and she said she'd collect
     Saturday. Wrote her name on a Post-it, tucked it inside the cover,
     set it under the counter. $16.99.

  5. Walking-past impulse: a man who "just came in for a look" and
     left with two books, slightly dazed, as though he hadn't quite
     meant to but was pleased about it. $24.00. This is the bookshop
     effect. I am not responsible for it. I merely facilitate it.

  6. Reading Circle flyer moment: a woman came in asking about events.
     Showed her the new flyers (still warm from the printer, nearly).

11:35 AM - 12:10 PM  [35 min] CONSIGNMENT AGREEMENT TEMPLATE
  I have been putting this off for two weeks. After the Ingram delivery
  was sorted and the shop was quiet, I sat down at the back desk and
  made myself do it.

  Opened Google Docs. Used the Booksellers Association template as a
  starting point (I'd downloaded it last month and done absolutely
  nothing with it). Adapted it for Page Turner Books.

  Final structure:
  ├─ Header: Page Turner Books Consignment Agreement
  ├─ Section 1: Parties (consignor and shop details)
  ├─ Section 2: Description of goods (title, ISBN, quantity, unit price)
  ├─ Section 3: Commission rate — 35% retained by shop, 65% to consignor
  ├─ Section 4: Display period — minimum 90 days
  ├─ Section 5: Sales reporting — monthly, by email
  ├─ Section 6: Payment terms — within 14 days of monthly report
  ├─ Section 7: Unsold stock — returned within 7 days of agreement end,
  │    at consignor's collection or cost
  ├─ Section 8: Liability — stock held at consignor's risk (standard)
  └─ Section 9: Termination — 30 days' notice either party

  Saved as "Page Turner Books — Consignment Agreement Template v1.0"
  in the shop's shared Google Drive folder.

  Two people from the newsletter are waiting on this. I'll email them
  both Monday with the template attached and ask them to review and
  return signed copies. It feels good to have it done. It's been
  quietly nagging at me every time I looked at my to-do list.
