08:25 AM - 09:12 AM  [47 min] QB ONLINE — BELLA'S FLORIST (SESSION 1)

  ⬛ QUICKBOOKS ONLINE SESSION 1  [08:25 AM – 09:12 AM, 47 min]

  Logged in to QBO at app.qbo.intuit.com. Clearwater has four company files
  active under our accountant login. Found Bella's Florist in the client list
  and clicked through.

  First thing I wanted to do: get to the Chart of Accounts. In Desktop I'd
  click Lists, then Chart of Accounts, and there it was — a clean flat list,
  sortable, editable. Here I had to remember: gear icon. Top right. I clicked
  it and got a dropdown with columns — "Your Company," "Lists," "Tools,"
  "Profile." I looked under Lists. I see "All Lists," "Products and Services,"
  "Recurring Transactions"... no Chart of Accounts. Clicked "All Lists." Still
  not immediately obvious. Finally found it — it's labeled "Chart of Accounts"
  under the Your Company column, not Lists. Wrote that down on my notepad in
  red: GEAR > YOUR COMPANY > CHART OF ACCOUNTS.

  Reviewed the COA for Bella's. Everything looked in order from last session.
  Checking 10100, Savings 10200, Accounts Receivable 11000, Inventory Asset
  13000, and so on. She runs about 40 accounts total, which is manageable.

  Now for the reconciliation. In Desktop, I'd go to Banking > Reconcile and
  a wizard would pop up asking for the ending balance and statement date. Clean.
  Logical. In QBO I clicked "Accounting" in the left navigation bar (which, to
  be fair, I've mostly memorized by now), then "Reconcile." Good — the
  Reconcile screen is actually not too bad. Entered statement date 03/31 and
  ending balance $14,208.37.

  That's where the trouble started. The screen showed a beginning balance of
  $11,942.14. My hand-written log says it should be $11,954.82. Twelve-dollar
  discrepancy. Before I panic, I need to check whether someone entered a
  transaction after the fact against February.

  Spent twenty minutes digging. Found it: a $12.68 bill payment to Sunshine
  Wholesale (vendor) had been entered by — well, by me — against 02/28 instead
  of 03/01. Easy fix in Desktop: open the check, change the date. In QBO I
  right-clicked... nothing. I had to find the transaction in the register. Went
  to Accounting > Chart of Accounts > 10100 Checking > View Register. Scrolled
  down. Found it. Clicked the little pencil/edit. Changed the date from 02/28
  to 03/01. Saved. QBO asked if I was sure because it would affect a prior
  period. I said yes, I'm sure. The beginning balance corrected to $11,954.82.
  Phew.

  Now I needed to connect the bank feed to pull in March transactions. In
  Desktop, I used to import a .QBO file by hand and it was perfectly reliable.
  QBO is supposed to auto-connect to Chase. The Banking tab showed Bella's
  Chase account as "connected" with a green dot, but when I hit "Update" to
  pull the March transactions, I got a spinning wheel for two full minutes and
  then: "Bank feed connection error. Please reconnect your account."

  I clicked "Reconnect." It took me to a screen asking me to log in to Chase
  directly. I don't have Patrice's Chase online banking credentials. I have to
  call her.

  Then I needed to enter the deposit to the bank account. In Desktop, I'd click
  Banking > Make Deposits, select all the Undeposited Funds items, and boom —

  At 11:15 AM I tried the bank feed update again. Same error. I clicked the
  small "?" help icon next to the error message. It opened an article about

  Decided to try importing a .CSV file instead — Chase lets you download
  transactions as a CSV. I don't have Patrice's Chase login to do this myself.
  Put another note in my call queue. In the meantime, I'll reconcile with what
  I have manually entered and flag any gaps.

  Back to reconciliation. Accounting > Reconcile > Bella's Florist Checking.
  Statement balance $14,208.37, statement date 03/31. QBO showed my entered
  transactions. I went through the cleared column, checking off items that
  matched the Chase PDF line by line.

  Cleared transactions:
    All eight vendor bills (payments made): matched Chase debits ✓
    The $7,870.07 deposit on 03/29: matched Chase credit ✓
    One item I didn't have: Chase bank service charge 03/31 $14.00 — forgot
      to enter this. Created a new transaction: Bank Charges expense, $14.00,
      03/31. Went back to reconcile screen.

  Difference now showing: -$0.00. Wait, no — $218.42 off. Something's wrong.

  Stared at the screen for ten minutes. Compared every line. Finally spotted
  it: I had entered the Clearwater Electric bill as $214.77 but the Chase
  statement shows $241.77. I transposed the digits. In Desktop I would just
  open the check and fix the amount. In QBO... I went to the reconcile screen,
  found the transaction, clicked it — it opened the original bill. I can edit
  the amount on an unreconciled bill. Changed $214.77 to $241.77. Went back
  to reconcile. The difference dropped to... -$26.27. Still off.

  Oh. The Sunshine Wholesale $712.30 — the smudged receipt. Patrice called
  back at 11:39 while I was still on this. She said it was $112.30, not $712.30.
  A $600.00 difference. I voided the original bill entry and re-entered it as
  $112.30. Voiding in QBO is slightly nerve-wracking — a pop-up warns you in
  big letters, which I appreciate, but it still gives me palpitations. The old
  Desktop software just let you delete quietly.

  Back to reconcile. Difference: $573.73 off in the other direction now.
  The $600 swap introduced a $573.73... wait, I'm confusing myself. Let me
  think. Original: $712.30. Correct: $112.30. Difference: $600.00 I over-
  entered. After voiding and re-entering: the reconciled total should go up by
  $600.00. But the statement balance hasn't changed. So the reconciled book
  balance should now be $600.00 closer to $14,208.37...

  At 11:48 I had to stop and eat lunch. Left the reconciliation tab open. The
  difference was $26.27 and I cannot figure out why. Printed a screen capture
  of the reconcile screen on my desk printer (yes, I print screen captures).
  Will revisit after lunch with fresh eyes.

END OF DAY — MARTHA'S REFLECTION
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  Well. It took longer than it should have, but Bella's Florist is done for
  March and the books balance to the penny. That always feels good, no matter
  what software you're using.

  The bank feed is my biggest headache with QBO. In Desktop, I imported a .QBO
  file from the bank and it just worked — no logins, no "OAuth errors," none
  of that. I had full control. Here I'm dependent on a connection that can
  apparently just... stop working. And I can't fix it without the client's bank
  credentials. That's a real problem for a bookkeeper. The client shouldn't
  have to reconnect their own bank feed. This feels like a design flaw to me,
  but maybe I'm missing something.

  The transposition error ($214.77 vs. $241.77) was my own fault and I'm
  annoyed with myself. I used to do triple-check entry in Desktop. I need to
  do that here too. Old habit worth keeping.

  One thing QBO did well today: the reconciliation screen is actually cleaner
  than I expected. Once I found the "Reconcile" option (under Accounting — not
  Banking, which is where I instinctively look), the interface for checking
  off cleared items is easy to read. The running difference counter at the top
  is helpful. I'll give it that.
