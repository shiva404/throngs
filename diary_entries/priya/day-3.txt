08:30 AM - 09:10 AM  [40 min] QB ONLINE — RECORDING MESA PACIFIC PAYMENT

  ⬛ QUICKBOOKS ONLINE SESSION 1  [08:32 AM – 09:09 AM, 37 min]

  I've been reading about this. My cheat sheet has the steps (from Raj's
  verbal explanation two weeks ago, and from the QB help article I read Monday
  evening). Let me do this carefully.

  Steps I followed:
    1. Sales > Customers > Mesa Pacific Goods > open customer record
    2. Looked at their open invoices — INV-2803 ($3,400.00) showing as overdue
    3. From the customer page I clicked "Receive Payment" (big green button
       at top right — one of the "friendly" ones I know means I'm in the right
       place)
    4. "Receive Payment" form opened. Fields:
         - Customer: Mesa Pacific Goods (auto-filled — correct)
         - Payment date: today, 03/12/2026 — I changed this to yesterday,
           03/11/2026, because the payment notification email said it was
           received yesterday. Raj once told me "always use the date the
           payment was actually received, not the date you're entering it."
           I remembered that.
         - Payment method: Check (payment notification said "check #4821")
         - Reference/Check #: 4821
         - Amount received: $3,400.00
         - Below: a list of open invoices for this customer. INV-2803 was
           the only one. It auto-populated the $3,400.00 against it.
           Amount applied: $3,400.00. Difference: $0.00.

  I stopped before saving and read through the entire form twice. The amount
  was right. The invoice was right. The date was right. The check number was
  filled in. The difference field showed $0.00 which I believe means the
  payment exactly matches the invoice, so nothing is left over and nothing
  is underpaid.

  Hovered over Save & Close for a moment. Thought: "Is there anything I'm
  forgetting?" Re-read Raj's instructions in my head. Looked at my cheat
  sheet. Everything lined up.

  Clicked Save & Close.

  INV-2803 is now showing as "Paid" in QB. I clicked on the invoice to verify
  and the big green "PAID" stamp is there. The payment is listed in the
  activity log at the bottom: "Payment received $3,400.00 on 03/11/2026."

  I logged the payment in Excel. I feel pretty good about that. It went
  exactly the way the cheat sheet said it would.

09:25 AM - 09:50 AM  [25 min] PREP + CALL TO SHORELINE FOOD SERVICE

  I sat with my notepad for a few minutes before calling. Wrote down:
    - INV number being disputed: INV-2855 ($1,100.00, sent Monday)
    - What I know: it was a routine entry, 2 line items
    - What I don't know: what their complaint is
    - What I'm allowed to do: listen, take notes, tell them I'll investigate
    - What I'm NOT allowed to do: promise credits, promise changes, agree to
      anything — escalate to Raj if it's an actual dispute

  Called the Shoreline main line at 9:31 AM. Got through to their AP contact,
  a woman named Karen. She was polite but clearly a little annoyed.

  The dispute: Shoreline received INV-2855 for $1,100.00. They expected
  it to be $980.00 based on a quote they have on file from our sales rep.
  The $120 difference is because they say they were quoted a lower unit price
  on one item. They're not refusing to pay — they just want clarification
  before they pay the full $1,100.

  I said: "I completely understand, Karen. I want to make sure this gets
  resolved correctly for you. Let me review the details on our end and I'll
  get back to you today if at all possible. Can I confirm the best number or
  email to reach you?" She gave me her email.

  After I hung up my hand was shaking a little. Not visibly — just that
  internal thing where your body is more stressed than you're letting on.

  I wrote everything down in my notepad immediately so I wouldn't forget.
  Key facts: $1,100 invoiced, $980 expected by customer, $120 discrepancy,
  their reference to a sales quote on file.

  I do not have access to the sales quoting system — that's outside my area.
  Even if I did, I wouldn't know whether the quote supersedes the sales order
  I was given. This is 100% an escalation.
