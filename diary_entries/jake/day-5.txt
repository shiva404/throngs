  Friday. I actually slept until 7:15. Felt alien.

  First thing (as promised to myself): look up deferred revenue.
  Not because anyone is asking today, but because I keep nodding slowly
  when investors say it and I'm running out of good nodding faces.

  Googled: "deferred revenue SaaS simple explanation"

  Okay. Here it is. When a customer pays you for 12 months upfront,
  you can't call the whole thing revenue on day one — you have to
  "recognize" it as revenue month by month as you deliver the service.
  The unearned portion sits on the balance sheet as a liability called
  "deferred revenue" because you technically owe them service you haven't
  delivered yet.

  This is... actually logical? If a customer paid us $24,000 for 12
  months and then cancelled in month 2, we'd owe them a refund for
  months 3-12. So yes. You're right to not count it all at once.

  Googled: "do we have deferred revenue if all customers pay monthly"

  Short answer: not really, or only a tiny bit (for invoices that cross
  month-end). That's us — 90% of our customers are on monthly subscriptions
  through Stripe. Almost no upfront annual payments yet. So deferred
  revenue is basically negligible for us right now.

  Filed this away. If we ever start selling annual contracts — which is
  actually a Q2 goal, annual prepay at discount — this becomes relevant.
  I'll tell Carol in advance so she can set it up correctly.

  I feel 12% smarter about accounting than I did on Monday. This is the
  most accounting knowledge I've absorbed in a single week since starting
  the company. I'm not sure if I should be proud of this or concerned.
