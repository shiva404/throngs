07:00 AM - 07:45 AM  [45 min] MORNING — GROSS MARGIN RESEARCH
  First thing. Before coffee. This is how important the gross margin
  situation is. I did not want to walk into a board call not knowing
  the one number Ashley flagged.

  Googled: "gross margin SaaS company formula"

  Okay. Gross Margin = (Revenue - Cost of Goods Sold) / Revenue x 100.

  For SaaS, COGS is: hosting/infrastructure, any costs directly tied to
  delivering the service. NOT payroll (usually). NOT rent. Those are
  operating expenses.

  Our COGS = cloud infrastructure (AWS): $8,100/month = $24,300 for Q1.
  Our Revenue (Q1, accrual): $57,300.
  Gross Profit: $57,300 - $24,300 = $33,000.
  Gross Margin: $33,000 / $57,300 = 57.6%.

  Is 57.6% good? Googled: "what is a good gross margin for B2B SaaS"

  Most sources say 70-85% is good for SaaS. 57% is below average.
  The main reason is probably that our AWS costs are high relative to
  revenue at this stage. As revenue scales, infrastructure costs as a
  percentage should drop.

  So my answer for the board: "Gross margin is currently 57%, which
  reflects our stage — infrastructure costs are relatively fixed and
  will compress as ARR scales. We expect to be at 70%+ by Series B."

  I practiced this sentence six times out loud. It sounds like I know
  what I'm talking about. Technically everything in it is true.

  Made coffee. Feeling dangerous.
