  ⬛ QUICKBOOKS ONLINE SESSION 2  [09:44 AM – 10:05 AM, 21 min]

  Came back after a quick bathroom break and opened the P&L report again
  to see if Carol's payroll fix had landed yet. It had not. But while
  looking at the report I noticed the "accounting method" toggle in the
  top right corner. It says "Accrual." There's a button to switch to "Cash."

  I switched it to Cash.

  Revenue jumped from $57,300 to $61,900. THERE IT IS. That's my number.
  That's what matches Stripe (roughly). Cash basis shows money when it
  actually lands in the bank. Accrual shows... something else. Something
  theoretical.

  Googled: "should i use cash or accrual basis quickbooks"
  Read the article. The short answer is: it depends, and for companies
  with investors, accrual is generally expected because it better matches
  revenue to when it was earned. For board reporting, use accrual.

  So the $57,300 is the "right" number for the board. I hate this.
  The $61,900 feels more real to me but apparently I shouldn't use it
  for the board deck. Sent Carol a voice note: "Hey, for the board deck
  am I supposed to use accrual or cash basis for the P&L? I switched it
  and the number is different and I need to know which one to put in
  the slide."

  Carol called back immediately (she never calls back immediately, this
